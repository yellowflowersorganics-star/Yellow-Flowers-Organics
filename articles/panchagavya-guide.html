üõ†Ô∏è Preparation Procedure (10-15 Days)

Days 1-3: Initial Ghee-Dung Fermentation

  1. Take a large plastic or earthen pot (50-100L capacity)
  2. Add 100 kg fresh cow dung
  3. Add 10 kg cow ghee to the dung
  4. Mix thoroughly with wooden stick
  5. Stir clockwise and anticlockwise for 3-5 minutes
  6. Do this twice daily (morning and evening) for 3 days
  7. Keep in shade, cover loosely with cloth

Why this step? Ghee-dung fermentation creates a probiotic base. Ghee acts as preservative and enhances microbial activity. This 3-day pre-fermentation is unique to Panchagavya!

Day 4: Adding All Liquid Ingredients

  1. Transfer the ghee-dung mixture to the 200L drum
  2. Add 60 liters cow urine
  3. Add 40 liters unboiled milk (fresh, not heated)
  4. Add 40 liters curd/yogurt
  5. Dissolve 20 kg jaggery in 20L warm water, add to drum
  6. Mash 240 ripe bananas into pulp, add to drum
  7. Add 60 liters tender coconut water (if available)
  8. Add remaining clean water to make up 200L total volume
  9. Mix everything thoroughly for 10-15 minutes
  10. Cover loosely with cloth (never seal!)

Days 5-15: Main Fermentation Period

  • Stir twice daily (morning and evening) for 5-10 minutes each
  • Mix both clockwise and anticlockwise directions
  • Keep in shaded, cool place away from direct sunlight
  • Optimal temperature: 25-35¬∞C (77-95¬∞F)
  • Days 5-7: Active fermentation, foam formation, strong smell develops
  • Days 8-12: Fermentation stabilizes, color becomes golden-brown
  • Days 13-15: Ready! Fermented smell (pleasant, not foul), uniform consistency

Ready Indicators: Golden-brown color, pleasant fermented smell, uniform consistency, 10-15 days passed

After Day 15: Storage & Usage

  • Panchagavya is ready after 10-15 days of fermentation
  • Can be stored for 1-2 months if stirred daily
  • Store in shade with loose cover
  • Always stir well before use - solids settle at bottom
  • Always filter through cloth before spraying
  • Quality improves with age up to 30 days
  • Use within 60 days for best microbial activity

Method: Dip seedling roots for 30 minutes before transplanting

Benefits: Reduces transplant shock, faster establishment, better survival rate

Dose: 3-5 liters per acre through fertigation tank

Preparation: Filter through fine cloth to prevent clogging

Timing: Apply during last 30 minutes of irrigation

F. Post-Harvest Soil Treatment

Dilution: 5% solution

Purpose: Replenish soil microbes, prepare for next crop

Application: Soil drench after harvest

‚úÖ Important Tips for Success

‚úÖ Do's

  • Always use clean, chlorine-free water for both preparation and dilution
  • Store in shaded place, cover loosely to allow airflow
  • Stir the concentrate daily even during storage (keeps microbes active)
  • Always filter through cloth before spraying to avoid nozzle clogging
  • Apply foliar sprays during cool hours (morning/evening)
  • Use fresh cow products - especially milk and curd should be fresh
  • Prefer desi cow products for better quality (but others work too)
  • Add tender coconut water if available - significantly boosts effectiveness
  • Continue stirring twice daily throughout fermentation
  • Test on a few plants first before large-scale application

‚ùå Don'ts

  • Don't use boiled milk - heat kills beneficial bacteria
  • Don't seal the container - microbes need oxygen for aerobic fermentation
  • Don't use chlorinated water - kills beneficial microorganisms
  • Don't spray during harsh midday sun - causes leaf burn
  • Don't mix with chemical fertilizers or pesticides
  • Don't skip the 3-day ghee-dung pre-fermentation - it's critical!
  • Don't use stale or spoiled cow products
  • Don't store in direct sunlight - UV rays damage microbes
  • Don't use metal containers - corrodes and affects pH
  • Don't skip daily stirring - fermentation will be incomplete

‚ö†Ô∏è Troubleshooting Common Issues

Problem: Foul Smell During Fermentation

Solution smells rotten (like sewage) instead of pleasant fermented smell.

Solution

Anaerobic fermentation due to sealing or insufficient stirring. Discard if very foul. For next batch: Cover loosely (never seal), stir twice daily without fail, use fresh ingredients, ensure good ventilation. The 3-day ghee-dung pre-fermentation prevents this.

Problem: Milk/Curd Spoiling Too Fast

Milk or curd going bad before fermentation completes.

Solution

Use fresh products only. The cow urine and ghee act as preservatives. Ensure proper fermentation temperature (not too hot). If living in very hot climate, reduce preparation time to 7-10 days instead of 15. Store in coolest shaded place available.

Problem: Sprayer Nozzle Clogging

Spray nozzles getting blocked frequently during application.

Solution

ALWAYS filter Panchagavya through 3-4 layers of cloth before adding to spray tank. Use only the filtered liquid, leave settled solids. For drip: use 200 mesh filter. Clean nozzles after each use. Consider diluting further (2% instead of 3%) for very fine spray nozzles.

Problem: Leaf Burn After Spraying

Plants showing brown spots or yellowing after Panchagavya application.

Solution

Applied during hot sun or too concentrated. Solution: 1) Spray only early morning/evening, 2) Reduce concentration to 2-3%, 3) Do patch test on few plants first, 4) Ensure proper dilution - measure accurately, 5) Add more water if in doubt.
