What is On-Farm Multiplication?

On-farm multiplication of beneficial microorganisms and bio-inputs involves producing various microbial solutions and bio-inputs using locally available resources. Instead of purchasing expensive ready-made products, farmers can create these essential inputs right on their farm, reducing costs while improving soil and plant health.

At Yellow Flowers Organic Farm, we practice comprehensive on-farm multiplication, producing everything from plant growth promoters to bio-fungicides using natural, locally-sourced materials. This approach has made us nearly self-sufficient in farm inputs while maintaining 100% organic certification.

üî¨ Multiplication Tips for Success

üß™ Mother Cultures

Use high-quality mother cultures for each bio-input, and multiply them in suitable growth mediums. Source authentic cultures from reputable suppliers or research institutions.

üßº Cleanliness

Ensure all equipment and containers are clean to avoid contamination. Wash containers with soap, rinse thoroughly, and air dry before use.

üå°Ô∏è Temperature Control

Maintain suitable temperatures for fermentation, usually between 20-35¬∞C (68-95¬∞F), to encourage microbial activity. Protect from extreme heat or cold.

üí® Oxygen Levels

Manage oxygen levels appropriately‚Äîmost beneficial microbes thrive in aerobic conditions, while others like those in Bokashi prefer anaerobic environments.

üåæ Local Resources

Utilize locally available materials such as plant leaves, fruit waste, animal manure, and natural soil to reduce costs and increase sustainability.

üìã Record Keeping

Maintain detailed records of preparation dates, ingredients, ratios, and results. This helps optimize your recipes and track effectiveness over time.
