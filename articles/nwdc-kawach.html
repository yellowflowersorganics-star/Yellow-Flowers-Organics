NWDC Kawach: Natural shield for plant protection and disease control

üõ°Ô∏è What is NWDC Kawach?

NWDC Kawach (Natural Waste Decomposer Culture) is a specially formulated bio-control agent developed by DKC/NCOF for plant protection, soil disease control, and natural pest resistance. The word "Kawach" means "shield" in Hindi, reflecting its protective function for plants.

Unlike OWDC, which primarily focuses on organic matter decomposition, Kawach is designed specifically to control soil-borne pathogens and enhance the plant's resistance against bacterial, viral, and fungal diseases. It's a probiotic microbial consortium that acts as a biological shield for your crops.

üß¨ Microbial Composition

NWDC Kawach contains a powerful consortium of beneficial microorganisms selected specifically for biocontrol:

Bacillus subtilis

Produces lipopeptides and antibiotics that suppress pathogens. Forms protective biofilms on roots. Induces systemic resistance in plants.

Pseudomonas fluorescens

Strong antagonist producing siderophores and HCN. Competes with pathogens for nutrients and space. Excellent root colonizer.

Trichoderma harzianum

Mycoparasite that attacks fungal pathogens. Produces cell wall-degrading enzymes. Improves plant growth and nutrient uptake.

Streptomyces spp.

Produces diverse antibiotics and antifungal compounds. Controls bacterial and fungal diseases. Improves soil structure.

Actinomycetes

Produces bioactive compounds. Suppresses soil-borne pathogens. Enhances decomposition of resistant organic matter.

Endophytic Microbes

Colonize plant tissues internally. Provide systemic protection against diseases. Enhance plant stress tolerance.

Why This Combination Works

This multi-species consortium works through multiple modes of action:

  • ‚úì Competition: Outcompete pathogens for nutrients and space
  • ‚úì Antibiosis: Produce antibiotics and antifungal compounds
  • ‚úì Parasitism: Directly attack and destroy pathogens
  • ‚úì Induced Resistance: Activate plant's own defense mechanisms
  • ‚úì Niche Exclusion: Occupy root surface, preventing pathogen colonization

üí° Pro Tip: Why Add Besan?

Besan (chickpea flour) provides protein and minerals that boost microbial growth, especially for Bacillus and Pseudomonas. It's optional but recommended for better results and faster multiplication.

üß™ Step-by-Step Multiplication Procedure

Day 0: Preparation & Inoculation

  1. Dissolve 2 kg jaggery in 10 liters of warm water (or use 4-5 L fresh sugarcane juice)
  2. If using besan, add 250-500g chickpea flour to the jaggery solution
  3. Stir well until completely dissolved, then let cool to room temperature
  4. Fill the drum with 190 liters of chlorine-free water
  5. Add the jaggery-besan solution to the drum
  6. Add 1 liter of Kawach culture
  7. Stir thoroughly for 5-7 minutes with a wooden stick
  8. Cover with muslin cloth or gunny bag (do not seal tight)

Temperature: Keep at 25-35¬∞C (77-95¬∞F) for optimal growth. Place in shaded, well-ventilated area.

Days 1‚Äì5/7: Fermentation Period

  • Stir twice daily (morning and evening) for 5 minutes each time
  • Keep drum in cool, shaded location with good ventilation
  • By Day 3-4, observe slight color change (brownish tint)
  • By Day 5-7, solution turns brown with fermented smell (earthy, not foul)
  • Surface may show slight foam or bubbles (good sign of microbial activity)
  • Check for pleasant, earthy smell (bad smell = contamination, discard)

Ready Indicators: Brown color, fermented earthy smell, slight foam on surface, cloudy appearance

After Day 5-7: Ready to Use

  • Solution is ready when it turns brown with fermented smell
  • Stir well before each use to distribute microbes evenly
  • Use within 30 days for maximum efficacy
  • Filter through muslin cloth if using for drip irrigation or foliar spray
  • Store remaining solution in shade with loose cover
  • Continue stirring daily even during storage period

üåø Application Methods

NWDC Kawach can be applied in multiple ways for comprehensive plant protection:

1. Soil Drenching

Method: Mix 2-4 L Kawach in 200 L water, apply directly to root zone

Rate: 20-25 L per acre via irrigation or manual drenching

Frequency: Every 15-30 days during growing season

Best For: Controlling soil-borne diseases, establishing root colonization

2. Seed Treatment

Method: Mix 200 mL Kawach in 5 L water, coat seeds or dip cuttings

Duration: 10-30 minutes soaking

Drying: Shade dry before planting

Best For: Protecting seeds from soil pathogens, improving germination

3. Drip Irrigation / Fertigation

Method: Apply through drip system or venturi injector

Rate: 10-15 L per acre (filter through cloth first)

Frequency: Every 2-3 weeks

Best For: Large-scale application, uniform distribution

4. Nursery Root Dipping

Method: Dip seedling roots in 2% solution (1:50 dilution) before transplanting

Duration: 10-30 minutes

Best For: Protecting transplants, reducing transplant shock, establishing beneficial microbes

5. Compost Fortification

Method: Spray 2 L Kawach over 1 ton compost during composting

Result: Creates disease-suppressive compost enriched with biocontrol agents

Best For: Making pathogen-free compost, adding biocontrol layer

6. Foliar Spray (Preventive)

Method: Mix 200 mL in 15 L water, spray on leaves

Frequency: Every 15-20 days (preventive measure)

Timing: Early morning or late evening

Best For: Preventing foliar diseases, systemic resistance

‚úÖ Important Tips for Success

‚úÖ Do's

  • Always use chlorine-free water (well water or let tap water stand 24 hours)
  • Store in shade at ambient temperature (15-35¬∞C)
  • Use within 30 days after preparation for best results
  • Stir solution regularly (daily if stored)
  • Filter before using in drip/fogger/foliar applications
  • Apply preventatively, not just curatively
  • Combine with other bio-inputs like OWDC and Trichoderma
  • Keep soil moist after application for better colonization
  • Apply during cooler parts of the day (morning/evening)
  • Maintain detailed application records

‚ùå Don'ts

  • Don't use tap water containing chlorine ‚Äì it kills beneficial microbes
  • Avoid direct sunlight during storage or application
  • Don't seal containers tightly ‚Äì microbes need air exchange
  • Don't store for more than a month after multiplication
  • Don't let solution sit untouched without stirring
  • Don't apply directly if visible particles present ‚Äì filter first
  • Never mix with chemical fungicides or bactericides
  • Don't apply during extreme heat or rain
  • Don't expect instant results ‚Äì allow 7-10 days for establishment
  • Don't use metal containers or tools ‚Äì plastic/wood only

‚ö†Ô∏è Troubleshooting Common Issues

Problem: Foul Smell

Solution smells rotten or putrid instead of earthy fermented smell.

Solution

Contamination occurred. Discard immediately. For next batch: ensure cleaner water, work in hygienic environment, stir more frequently (3x daily), and check culture quality.

Problem: No Color Change

After 7 days, solution is still clear without browning.

Solution

Low microbial activity. Check temperature (should be 25-35¬∞C), add 500g more jaggery, increase besan to 500g, stir 3x daily, and verify culture is not expired.

Problem: Diseases Still Occurring

Applied Kawach but still seeing disease symptoms.

Solution

Kawach works preventatively. If disease is severe, increase frequency to weekly, ensure soil moisture, check if chemicals were used recently (wait 15 days), combine with Trichoderma, and consider crop rotation.

Problem: Drip System Clogging

Emitters getting blocked when using Kawach in drip system.

Solution

Always filter through 3-4 layers of muslin cloth before adding to drip tank. Use 120-mesh screen filter. Clean system after each use. Flush lines with clean water.
