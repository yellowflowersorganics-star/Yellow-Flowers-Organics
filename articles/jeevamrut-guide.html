Jeevamrut: Nature's complete tonic for soil and plants

üêÑ What is Jeevamrut?

Jeevamrut (meaning "liquid of life" or "nectar of life" in Sanskrit) is a fermented microbial bio-culture prepared using cow dung, cow urine, jaggery, pulse flour, and native soil. It's one of the most important and cost-effective bio-inputs in organic farming, developed as part of traditional Indian agricultural practices.

At Yellow Flowers Organic Farm, Jeevamrut is our cornerstone bio-input. We apply it to every crop, every 15-20 days throughout the growing season. It's truly a complete package - fertilizer, growth promoter, microbial activator, and immunity booster - all in one!

üåü The Magic of Five Ingredients

üêÆ

Fresh Cow Dung (10 kg)

Rich in beneficial microorganisms (billions per gram), organic matter, and enzymes. Desi cow dung is preferred for its microbial diversity and quality.

üíß

Cow Urine (10 L)

Contains nitrogen, growth hormones, enzymes, and acts as a natural solvent. Has mild antimicrobial properties that prevent pathogenic growth.

üçØ

Jaggery (2 kg)

Food source (carbon) for microbes. Promotes rapid microbial multiplication during fermentation. Can be replaced with molasses.

üåæ

Protein and nitrogen source for microbes. Gram flour (besan) is most commonly used. Stimulates beneficial bacterial growth.

üå±

Native Soil (1-2 kg)

Introduces local beneficial microbes, especially from soil under healthy trees or bunds. Acts as microbial inoculant.

üí° Why Desi Cow Products?

Indigenous (desi) cow breeds like Gir, Sahiwal, Red Sindhi are preferred because:

  • ‚úì Their dung has higher microbial diversity and quality
  • ‚úì Contains more beneficial bacteria strains
  • ‚úì Higher enzyme content
  • ‚úì Cow urine has better hormonal profile
  • ‚úì However, crossbred or local cow products also work well!

üßë‚Äçüî¨ Preparation Procedure

Day 0: Mixing All Ingredients

  1. Fill water: Take 180-200 liters of clean, chlorine-free water in the drum
  2. Add cow dung: Add 10 kg fresh cow dung to the water
  3. Stir thoroughly: Mix well using wooden stick until dung disperses evenly (5-7 minutes)
  4. Add cow urine: Pour in 10 liters of fresh cow urine, mix well
  5. Add jaggery: Dissolve 2 kg jaggery in 2-3 liters warm water, add to drum
  6. Add pulse flour: Add 2 kg gram flour (besan), stir to prevent lumps
  7. Add native soil: Add 1-2 kg soil from under a healthy tree or field bund
  8. Stir clockwise & anti-clockwise: Mix thoroughly for 10-15 minutes in both directions
  9. Cover loosely: Cover with jute sack or loose cloth (allow air exchange, prevent insects)

Important: Do not seal the drum - aerobic fermentation requires oxygen!

Days 1-5/7: Fermentation Period

  • Stir twice daily: Morning and evening, 5 minutes each time (critical step!)
  • Stirring direction: Mix both clockwise and anti-clockwise
  • Keep in shaded area away from direct sunlight
  • Optimal temperature: 25-35¬∞C (77-95¬∞F)
  • Day 1-2: Observe bubbling and foam formation (active fermentation)
  • Day 3-4: Strong fermented smell develops (pleasant, not rotten)
  • Day 5-7: Fermentation completes, foam reduces, ready to use
  • Solution becomes brownish with cow dung solids settling at bottom

Ready Indicators: Pleasant fermented smell, reduced foam, brownish color, 5-7 days passed

After Day 5-7: Application

  • Jeevamrut is ready for use after 3-7 days of fermentation
  • Best efficacy: Use between Day 3-7 (peak microbial activity)
  • Can be stored up to 7-10 days with continued daily stirring
  • Stir well before use - solids settle at bottom
  • Filter through cloth if using for drip irrigation
  • For soil drench: Can use as-is without filtering
  • Do not store beyond 10 days - microbial quality deteriorates

üöú Application Methods

Jeevamrut is incredibly versatile and can be applied in multiple ways:

1. Soil Drenching (Primary Method)

Dose: 200 liters per acre per application

Method: Apply with irrigation water, preferably through open channel

Frequency: Every 15-30 days during vegetative and flowering stages

Purpose: Enrich soil microbes, improve nutrient availability, boost soil health

2. Drip Irrigation

Dose: 200 liters per acre through venturi or fertigation tank

Preparation: Filter through cloth to remove solids (prevent clogging)

Timing: Apply during last 30-45 minutes of irrigation

Best for: Vegetables, fruit crops with drip systems

3. Foliar Spray

Dilution: 5-10 liters Jeevamrut in 100 liters water

Timing: Early morning or late evening

Frequency: Every 15-20 days during vegetative growth

Purpose: Quick nutrient boost, foliar absorption, disease suppression

4. Seed Treatment

Method: Soak seeds in diluted Jeevamrut (1:10) for 30 minutes

Drying: Shade dry before sowing

Benefits: Better germination, vigorous seedlings, early microbial colonization

Works for: All seeds - vegetables, cereals, pulses

5. Nursery/Transplant Drenching

Before transplanting: Drench nursery bed with diluted solution (1:5)

After transplanting: Apply 200-300 ml per plant at root zone

Benefits: Reduces transplant shock, faster establishment, better survival rate

6. Fruit Tree Application

Dose: 5-10 liters per tree (depending on size)

Method: Apply in root zone basin

Frequency: Monthly during growing season

Works for: Mango, citrus, guava, pomegranate, etc.

‚úÖ Important Tips for Success

‚úÖ Do's

  • Always use fresh cow dung and urine - collected same day or within 24 hours
  • Stir twice daily without fail - increases oxygen and microbial activity
  • Prepare in shade - direct sunlight kills microbes
  • Use plastic or cement containers - avoid metal (reacts with urine)
  • Filter before using in drip or spray - prevents clogging
  • Use within 7 days of preparation for best microbial activity
  • Use native soil from healthy, pesticide-free areas
  • Apply preferably with irrigation for better soil distribution
  • Continue stirring daily even if not used immediately
  • Combine with other bio-inputs like Trichoderma, NPK bacteria for enhanced effect

‚ùå Don'ts

  • Don't use stale or stored cow dung/urine - microbial quality decreases
  • Don't seal the container - microbes need oxygen for aerobic fermentation
  • Don't use chlorinated tap water - kills beneficial microbes
  • Don't prepare in direct sunlight - UV rays damage microorganisms
  • Don't use metal containers or stirrers - corrodes, affects pH
  • Don't skip daily stirring - fermentation will be incomplete
  • Don't store for more than 10 days - microbes die, quality deteriorates
  • Don't mix with chemical fertilizers or pesticides
  • Don't use soil from chemically treated fields
  • Don't expect instant results - benefits accumulate over time

‚ö†Ô∏è Troubleshooting Common Issues

Problem: Foul/Rotten Smell

Solution smells bad (rotten eggs, sewage) instead of pleasant fermented smell.

Solution

Anaerobic fermentation occurred (lack of oxygen). Causes: 1) Container sealed too tightly, 2) Not stirred regularly. Solution: Discard if very foul. For next batch: Cover loosely, stir twice daily without fail, ensure good air exchange.

Problem: No Bubbling/Fermentation

After 2-3 days, no foam or bubbles visible, no fermentation smell.

Solution

Microbial activity is low. Causes: 1) Cow dung/urine not fresh, 2) Chlorinated water used, 3) Temperature too low. Solution: Add 1 kg more jaggery, stir vigorously 3x daily, move to warmer location (25-35¬∞C), ensure cow products are fresh.

Problem: Plants Not Responding

Applied Jeevamrut but no visible improvement in plant growth.

Solution

Jeevamrut works gradually, not instantly. Takes 2-3 applications (4-6 weeks) to see results. Check: 1) Are you using fresh solution (3-7 days old)?, 2) Stirring twice daily during preparation?, 3) Adequate dose (200L/acre)?, 4) Regular applications every 15-20 days? Be patient - benefits accumulate over time!

Problem: Drip System Clogging

Emitters getting blocked when using Jeevamrut through drip.

Solution

Solid particles blocking emitters. Solution: 1) Filter through 3-4 layers of cloth before adding to drip tank, 2) Use only the liquid portion, leave settled solids, 3) Flush system with clean water after Jeevamrut application, 4) Use screen filter (120 mesh minimum), 5) For very fine drip, dilute 1:1 with water.
