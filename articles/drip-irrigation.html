Drip Irrigation & Water Conservation

Precision Water Delivery for Maximum Efficiency

üíß

Drip irrigation saves water while improving crop productivity

The Water Challenge in Agriculture

Agriculture consumes approximately 70% of global freshwater resources, yet conventional irrigation methods waste 50-60% of applied water through evaporation, runoff, and deep percolation. Drip irrigation offers a sustainable solution, delivering water directly to plant roots with 90-95% efficiency.

üíß What is Drip Irrigation?

Drip irrigation is a micro-irrigation system that delivers water slowly and precisely to the root zone through a network of pipes, tubes, and emitters. This targeted approach minimizes water waste and optimizes plant growth.

Key Advantages

  • Water Savings: 40-70% less water compared to flood irrigation
  • Higher Yields: 20-90% increase depending on crop and conditions
  • Fertilizer Efficiency: Fertigation delivers nutrients directly to roots
  • Weed Reduction: Only crop area receives water, not weeds
  • Disease Prevention: Dry foliage reduces fungal diseases
  • Labor Savings: Automated system reduces manual work
  • Energy Efficiency: Low pressure requirements

üîß Components of Drip Irrigation System

1. Water Source

Well, borewell, pond, or tank with adequate water supply

2. Pump Unit

Provides necessary pressure (1-2 kg/cm¬≤ for most systems)

3. Filtration System

Essential to prevent emitter clogging - sand filter, screen filter, or disc filter

4. Fertilizer Tank (Optional)

Venturi injector or fertilizer tank for fertigation

5. Main Pipeline

PVC pipes (63-90mm diameter) distribute water from source

6. Sub-main Lines

Smaller PVC pipes (50-63mm) distribute to field sections

7. Lateral Lines

PE (polyethylene) pipes (12-16mm) with emitters

8. Emitters/Drippers

Release water at controlled rates (2-8 LPH typically)

üìê System Design Considerations

Emitter Spacing

Closely Spaced Crops

Vegetables: 30-45cm spacing between emitters

Row Crops

Cotton, Sugarcane: 60-75cm spacing

Orchards

Fruit trees: 2-4 emitters per tree in ring

Emitter Flow Rates

  • 2 LPH: Vegetables, flowers, greenhouse crops
  • 4 LPH: Field crops, medium spacing
  • 8 LPH: Orchards, widely spaced crops

Lateral Line Spacing

  • Sandy soil: Closer spacing (30-50cm)
  • Loamy soil: Medium spacing (50-75cm)
  • Clay soil: Wider spacing (75-100cm)

Calculating Water Requirement

Formula: Daily water need = Crop water requirement √ó Area √ó Crop factor

Example for Tomato (1 acre):

  • Crop water requirement: 5mm/day (peak season)
  • Area: 4000 m¬≤ (1 acre)
  • Crop coefficient: 1.15 (peak growth)

Daily need = 5 √ó 4000 √ó 1.15 = 23,000 liters

If 2000 emitters @ 2 LPH: Running time = 23,000 / (2000 √ó 2) = ~6 hours

Irrigation Frequency

  • Sandy soil: Daily irrigation, shorter duration
  • Loamy soil: Alternate day or every 2-3 days
  • Clay soil: Every 3-5 days, longer duration
  • Monsoon season: Reduce frequency based on rainfall

üåø Fertigation: Fertilizer Through Drip System

Benefits of Fertigation

  • 50-60% reduction in fertilizer use
  • Uniform nutrient distribution
  • Improved nutrient use efficiency
  • No labor for fertilizer application
  • No soil disturbance

Water-Soluble Organic Fertilizers We Use

  • Jeevamrut: Diluted and filtered
  • Panchagavya: 2-3% solution
  • Vermiwash: Directly applicable
  • Seaweed extract: Growth promoter
  • Humic acid: Improves nutrient uptake

Important: Always filter organic fertilizers through fine cloth (100 mesh) before injecting into drip system to prevent clogging.

üîß Maintenance Guide

Daily Maintenance

  • Check for leaks in pipes and fittings
  • Verify emitter operation (random sampling)
  • Monitor pressure gauge readings
  • Clean filter if pressure drops

Weekly Maintenance

  • Backwash sand filter
  • Clean screen filter thoroughly
  • Check fertilizer injection system
  • Inspect for animal damage to pipes

Monthly Maintenance

  • Flush all lateral lines
  • Clean clogged emitters with dilute acid (if needed)
  • Check all valves and replace damaged ones
  • Inspect pump and motor

Seasonal Maintenance

  • Chemical cleaning of entire system
  • Replace damaged lateral lines
  • Service pump and motor
  • Update irrigation schedule for season

üí° Water Conservation Tips

Combining Strategies

  1. Mulching: Reduces evaporation by 50-70%
  2. Rainwater harvesting: Capture and store rainwater
  3. Pond storage: Store water for lean periods
  4. Drip + Mulch: Most efficient combination
  5. Crop selection: Choose drought-tolerant varieties
  6. Timing: Irrigate early morning or evening
