üåø What is Neem/Garlic Bio-Insecticide?

Neem/Garlic Bio-Insecticide is a powerful organic pest control solution made from two of nature's most effective pest-repelling plants: Neem (Azadirachta indica) and Garlic (Allium sativum). This combination creates a broad-spectrum bio-insecticide that is safe, effective, and sustainable.

At Yellow Flowers Organic Farm, this is our primary defense against insect pests. We've been able to replace 70-80% of chemical insecticides with this natural alternative, protecting our crops while maintaining ecological balance.

üî¨ How Neem + Garlic Work Together

üå≥ Neem's Action

  • Azadirachtin - Disrupts insect growth and molting
  • Antifeedant - Stops pests from eating
  • Repellent - Keeps pests away
  • Oviposition deterrent - Prevents egg-laying
  • Systemic - Absorbed by plants for internal protection

üßÑ Garlic's Action

  • Allicin - Strong repellent compound
  • Contact killer - Damages soft-bodied insects
  • Fumigant - Volatile oils repel pests
  • Antibacterial - Prevents secondary infections
  • Masking - Hides plant odors from pests

Together, they provide: Multiple modes of action that prevent pest resistance, broader spectrum control, and enhanced efficacy!

üí° Why Fermented Cow Urine?

Fermented cow urine (Gomutra) enhances the bio-insecticide by:

  • ‚úì Acting as a natural solvent to extract neem compounds
  • ‚úì Adding beneficial microorganisms
  • ‚úì Improving plant absorption (acts as a spreader)
  • ‚úì Providing trace nutrients and growth hormones
  • ‚úì Enhancing the repellent effect

üßë‚Äçüî¨ Multiplication Procedure (10-15 Days)

Day 0: Preparation

  1. Prepare neem: Crush 5 kg fresh neem leaves or grind dried neem kernels into coarse powder
  2. Prepare garlic: Crush 500g-1kg garlic cloves into a paste (use mortar/pestle or grinder)
  3. Mix in container: Add crushed neem and garlic paste to the 50-60L drum
  4. Add cow urine: Pour in 10 liters of fermented cow urine
  5. Add water: Fill with 40 liters of chlorine-free water
  6. Add jaggery: Dissolve 1 kg jaggery in 2L warm water, add to drum (promotes fermentation)
  7. Stir thoroughly: Mix all ingredients for 10 minutes with wooden stick
  8. Cover: Use muslin cloth to cover (allow air exchange, keep insects out)

Days 1‚Äì15: Fermentation Period

  • Stir daily: Mix once or twice daily using wooden stick (5 minutes each time)
  • Keep in shaded area away from direct sunlight
  • Temperature: Maintain 25-35¬∞C (77-95¬∞F) for optimal fermentation
  • Observe color change: Solution will darken (brown to dark brown)
  • Smell: Strong pungent odor develops (neem + garlic + fermentation)
  • Surface: May show slight foam or scum (normal fermentation)
  • By Day 10-15: Strong smell, dark color, fully fermented

After Day 10-15: Straining & Storage

  • Strain the liquid: Filter through 2-3 layers of muslin cloth to remove solid particles
  • Press residue: Squeeze cloth to extract maximum liquid from solid residue
  • Store concentrate: Transfer filtered liquid to clean, dark plastic bottles or containers
  • Label clearly: Mark as "Neem-Garlic Concentrate" with preparation date
  • Storage location: Cool, shaded place away from direct sunlight
  • Shelf life: Up to 30 days if stored properly
  • Solid residue: Can be composted or applied to soil (still has pest-repellent properties)

üß™ Simple Spray Preparation (Without Fermentation)

For immediate use, you can also prepare a quick neem-garlic spray using neem oil:

Quick Spray Method (For 100 Liters Water)

  1. Neem Oil: 300-500 ml (preferably 85% azadirachtin or higher)
  2. Garlic Paste: 500g fresh garlic crushed into paste
  3. Emulsifier: 50-100 ml liquid soap or surf (helps oil mix with water)
  4. Water: 100 liters (chlorine-free)

Mixing Instructions:

  1. First mix neem oil with emulsifier (soap) in a small container - stir vigorously
  2. Add this mixture to 10 liters of water, stir well to form emulsion
  3. Add garlic paste, mix thoroughly
  4. Pour this into the spray tank with remaining 90 liters water
  5. Stir continuously for 5 minutes until uniform milky emulsion forms
  6. Use immediately (do not store diluted spray)

Note: The fermented method is more effective and economical for regular farm use!

üöú Field Application Methods

1. Foliar Spray (Primary Method)

Dilution: 5-10 liters fermented concentrate per 100 liters water

Application: Spray on both sides of leaves, stems, and visible pests

Coverage: Ensure thorough wetting of all plant surfaces

Volume: 200-400 L spray solution per acre depending on crop size

2. Preventive Application

Timing: Apply before pest appearance or at first signs

Frequency: Every 10-15 days throughout crop cycle

Dilution: Use lower concentration (5L per 100L water)

Purpose: Maintain pest pressure below economic threshold

3. Curative Application

Timing: When pest infestation is visible and increasing

Frequency: Every 5-7 days for 2-3 consecutive sprays

Dilution: Use higher concentration (10L per 100L water)

Target: Focus on heavily infested plants and surrounding areas

4. Soil Drench

Purpose: Control soil-borne pests and nematodes

Dilution: 10-15 L concentrate per 200L water

Application: Apply near root zone, 500ml per plant

Frequency: Once every 20-30 days

5. Seed Treatment

Purpose: Protect seeds from soil pests during germination

Method: Soak seeds in 1:10 dilution for 30 minutes

Drying: Shade dry seeds before sowing

Works for: Vegetables, pulses, cereals

üïê Best Application Timing

  • Early Morning: 6:00 AM - 9:00 AM (best time - minimal bee activity, cooler temperature)
  • Late Evening: After 4:30 PM - sunset (second best - protects beneficial insects)
  • Avoid: Midday (10 AM - 4 PM) - causes leaf burn, harms bees, rapid evaporation
  • Weather: Apply on calm days (no wind), avoid if rain expected within 6 hours

‚úÖ Important Tips for Success

‚úÖ Do's

  • Apply during early morning or late evening to protect bees
  • Use clean, chlorine-free water for better efficacy
  • Store concentrate away from sunlight in cool place
  • Do a patch test on a few plants before large-scale application
  • Spray both sides of leaves - pests often hide on undersides
  • Ensure thorough coverage - spray until solution drips off leaves
  • Use immediately after diluting concentrate
  • Filter solution before adding to sprayer to prevent clogging
  • Wear protective gear (mask, gloves) during application
  • Rotate with other bio-insecticides to prevent resistance

‚ùå Don'ts

  • Never mix with copper-based fungicides - reduces effectiveness
  • Don't mix with strong chemical pesticides
  • Don't spray during midday hot sun - causes leaf burn
  • Don't use hard or muddy water - affects emulsion
  • Don't store diluted spray - use fresh each time
  • Don't apply if rain is expected within 6 hours
  • Don't spray during flowering peak when bees are active
  • Don't exceed recommended dilution - doesn't improve control
  • Don't skip the emulsifier when using neem oil - won't mix properly
  • Don't expect instant kill like chemicals - works over 3-7 days

‚ö†Ô∏è Troubleshooting Common Issues

Problem: Leaf Burn After Spraying

Plants showing brown spots or yellowing after neem/garlic application.

Solution

Caused by spraying during hot sun or over-concentration. Solution: 1) Spray only in early morning/late evening, 2) Reduce concentration by 30-50%, 3) Do patch test first, 4) Add more water to dilute, 5) Avoid spraying tender new growth directly.

Problem: Poor Pest Control

Applied spray but pests are still present in high numbers after 7-10 days.

Solution

Multiple possible causes: 1) Increase concentration (use 10L per 100L), 2) Improve coverage - spray leaf undersides, 3) Spray every 5-7 days instead of 10-15, 4) Check if concentrate is fresh (use within 30 days), 5) Ensure thorough wetting - spray until dripping, 6) For severe infestation, use spot treatment with stronger dose.

Problem: Oil Not Mixing with Water

Neem oil floating on water surface, not forming emulsion.

Solution

Emulsification problem. Fix: 1) Add 50-100ml liquid soap/emulsifier, 2) First mix oil with soap separately, 3) Add this mixture to small amount of water (10L), stir vigorously, 4) Then add to full tank, 5) Use warm water (not hot) for better mixing, 6) Stir continuously for 5-10 minutes.

Problem: Foul Smell During Fermentation

Fermentation smells rotten instead of pungent neem-garlic smell.

Solution

Contamination or anaerobic fermentation. Discard and restart. For next batch: 1) Use clean container, 2) Use fresh ingredients, 3) Stir daily without fail, 4) Keep covered with cloth (not sealed), 5) Place in well-ventilated area, 6) Check water quality.
