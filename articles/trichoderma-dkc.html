üçÑ What is Tricho DKC?

Tricho-DKC is a specialized bio-fungicide formulated by Dr. Krishan Chandra, containing multiple strains of beneficial Trichoderma fungi. This liquid formulation is designed to control soil-borne and foliar fungal diseases while simultaneously promoting plant growth and enhancing nutrient utilization.

At Yellow Flowers Organic Farm, Tricho-DKC has become our primary defense against fungal diseases. Unlike chemical fungicides that leave harmful residues, Trichoderma works with nature to protect our crops while improving soil health.

This multi-strain approach provides broader spectrum protection against various fungal diseases.

üß™ Multiplication Procedure (7‚Äì10 Days)

Day 0: Preparation & Inoculation

  1. Dissolve 2 kg jaggery (or molasses) in 10 liters of lukewarm water
  2. Add 50g yeast powder if using (optional but recommended - boosts growth)
  3. Stir until completely dissolved, let cool to room temperature
  4. Fill the drum with 190 liters of chlorine-free water
  5. Add the jaggery solution to the drum
  6. Add 1 bottle of Tricho-DKC mother culture
  7. Stir thoroughly for 5-7 minutes with wooden stick
  8. Cover with muslin cloth or loose lid (never seal tight)

Important: Maintain temperature between 20-30¬∞C (68-86¬∞F). Too hot kills the fungi, too cold slows growth.

Days 1‚Äì7: Fermentation & Growth

  • Stir twice daily (morning and evening) for 5 minutes each
  • Place drum in shaded, well-ventilated area
  • Observe for greenish surface growth by Day 4-5 (indicates Trichoderma colonization)
  • Check for pleasant, earthy mushroom-like smell
  • Monitor pH if possible (should be 5.5-7.0)
  • If contamination appears (bad smell, unusual colors), discard and start fresh

Good Signs: Greenish powder on surface, earthy smell, slightly cloudy liquid

Day 7-10: Ready to Use

  • Solution is ready when greenish spore mass is visible on surface
  • The liquid may be cloudy with floating greenish particles
  • Stir well before use to distribute spores evenly
  • Use within 15-20 days for maximum efficacy
  • Filter through cloth if using for foliar spray or drip (to prevent clogging)
  • Store remaining solution in shade with loose cover

üåæ Alternative: Solid Substrate Multiplication (Grain-Based)

For those who prefer solid substrate multiplication (more advanced):

Grain-Based Multiplication (Rice/Wheat)

  1. Prepare grains: Soak 5 kg rice or wheat in water for 6-8 hours
  2. Cook: Boil for 15-20 minutes until grains are soft but not mushy
  3. Drain & cool: Spread on clean cloth, allow to cool completely
  4. Bag: Fill polypropylene bags (1 kg per bag), don't pack tight
  5. Sterilize: Steam in pressure cooker for 30 minutes at 15 psi
  6. Cool & inoculate: Let bags cool, add 50ml Tricho culture per bag
  7. Incubate: Keep at 25-28¬∞C for 10-14 days until green growth covers grains
  8. Use: Mix directly into soil (100-200g per plant) or extract in water

Yield: 5 kg colonized grain from 50ml culture

üåø Application Methods & Rates

Tricho-DKC can be applied in multiple ways depending on your crop and disease pressure.

1. Soil Treatment

How to Apply: Apply directly to soil during bed preparation or through drip irrigation

Rate: 300 liters per acre, diluted with irrigation water

Timing: Before planting, at transplanting, and every 30-45 days

Purpose: Establishes beneficial Trichoderma in soil, protects roots from soil-borne pathogens

2. Seed Treatment

How to Apply: Soak seeds in diluted Tricho-DKC (1:10 ratio) for 20-30 minutes before planting

Rate: 50 ml culture in 500 ml water per kg of seeds

Drying: Shade dry seeds before sowing

Purpose: Protects seeds from fungal infections during germination, improves seedling vigor

3. Foliar Spray

How to Apply: Spray diluted solution on leaves, stems, and fruits

Rate: 100 liters per acre (filter through cloth first)

Frequency: Every 15-20 days or at first sign of disease

Timing: Early morning or late evening, avoid hot midday sun

Purpose: Controls foliar fungal diseases like powdery mildew, early blight

4. Seedling Dip

How to Apply: Dip seedling roots in Tricho solution before transplanting

Rate: 200 ml culture per liter of water

Duration: Dip for 10-15 minutes

Purpose: Protects transplants from transplant shock and root diseases

5. Compost Enrichment

How to Apply: Mix Tricho solution into compost during preparation

Rate: 5-10 liters per ton of compost

Benefit: Enriches compost with beneficial fungi, faster decomposition, disease-suppressive compost

‚úÖ Tips for Success

‚úÖ Do's

  • Apply preventatively before disease symptoms appear for best results
  • Use chlorine-free water (let tap water stand 24 hours or use well water)
  • Maintain sterile conditions during multiplication to prevent contamination
  • Stir solution daily during multiplication to ensure even growth
  • Store in cool, shaded location away from direct sunlight
  • Apply during early morning or late evening for foliar sprays
  • Keep soil moist after application - Trichoderma thrives in moist conditions
  • Filter solution before using in drip or sprayer to prevent clogging
  • Check pH during multiplication (ideal: 5.5-7.0)
  • Use within 15-20 days of multiplication for maximum efficacy

‚ùå Don'ts

  • Never mix with chemical fungicides - they kill beneficial fungi
  • Don't apply during extreme heat or direct midday sun
  • Don't use chlorinated water - chlorine kills Trichoderma
  • Don't seal containers tightly during multiplication - fungi need oxygen
  • Don't apply to waterlogged soil - Trichoderma needs aeration
  • Don't skip daily stirring during multiplication
  • Don't use after solution develops foul smell (indicates contamination)
  • Don't use metal containers or stirrers - plastic or wood only
  • Don't apply just after chemical fungicide use (wait 10-15 days)
  • Don't expect instant results - Trichoderma needs 5-7 days to establish

‚ö†Ô∏è Troubleshooting Common Issues

Problem: Wrong Color Growth

Instead of green, you see white, black, or blue mold growing during multiplication.

Solution

Contamination has occurred. Discard immediately. For next batch: ensure all equipment is clean, use fresh chlorine-free water, work in cleaner environment, and check mother culture quality.

Problem: No Visible Growth

After 7-10 days, no greenish growth is visible on the surface.

Solution

Check temperature (should be 20-30¬∞C), ensure daily stirring is done, verify mother culture is not expired, add 500g more jaggery if needed, and increase stirring frequency to 3x daily.

Problem: Disease Not Controlled

Applied Tricho but disease is still spreading.

Solution

Tricho works preventatively. If disease is already severe, increase application frequency to every 7 days, ensure soil is moist (not waterlogged), check if chemical fungicides were used recently (wait 15 days), and consider using fresh culture with higher concentration.

Problem: Clogged Sprayer/Drip

Nozzles getting blocked when using Trichoderma in spray or drip systems.

Solution

Always filter through 2-3 layers of muslin cloth before application. For drip, use screen filter (120 mesh minimum). Clean filters after each use.
