üß™ What is OWDC?

Organic Waste Decomposer (OWDC) is a microbial consortium developed by the National Centre of Organic Farming (NCOF), India. It contains seven strains of beneficial bacteria (including Lactobacillus, Bacillus, and others) that decompose organic waste, convert it into compost rapidly, and improve soil microbial diversity.

At Yellow Flowers Organic Farm, OWDC has become one of our most valuable bio-inputs. We use it for composting, soil enrichment, residue management, and even as a foliar spray. Its versatility and effectiveness make it indispensable for organic farming.

üß™ Multiplication Procedure (7‚Äì10 Day Fermentation)

Day 0: Preparation

  1. Dissolve 2 kg jaggery in 10 liters warm water (or use 3‚Äì5 L sugarcane juice)
  2. Add 1 bottle OWDC (DKC) culture to the jaggery mix
  3. Fill chlorine-free water up to 200 L in the plastic drum
  4. Stir thoroughly with a wooden stick for 3-5 minutes
  5. Cover with a muslin cloth or loose lid (don't seal tight)

Days 1‚Äì10: Fermentation

  • Stir twice daily (morning and evening) for 5 minutes
  • Place drum in a shaded, ventilated place
  • Observe foamy surface and fermented smell by Day 7‚Äì10
  • The solution is ready when it has a pleasant fermented smell (not rotten)
  • White foam or bubbles on surface indicate active fermentation

After Day 10: Ready to Use

  • Solution can be used immediately or stored up to 25-30 days
  • Filter through cloth before foliar spray or drip application
  • Store in shade with loose lid
  • Use within 30 days for best results

‚úÖ Important Tips for Success

‚úÖ Do's

  • Use clean, chlorine-free water (let tap water stand 24 hours or use well water)
  • Stir solution daily during fermentation to ensure proper aeration
  • Use within 25‚Äì30 days of preparation for maximum effectiveness
  • Store in shaded, cool location away from direct sunlight
  • Filter solution before foliar or drip application to prevent clogging
  • Maintain proper jaggery ratio (2 kg per 200 L) for optimal microbial growth
  • Cover drum with cloth to allow air exchange while preventing contamination
  • Keep detailed records of preparation and application dates

‚ùå Don'ts

  • Never use chlorinated or unclean water ‚Äì it kills beneficial microbes
  • Don't seal drum tightly ‚Äî microbes need oxygen for growth
  • Don't apply after the solution starts rotting (foul smell indicates contamination)
  • Don't keep in direct sunlight ‚Äì UV rays kill microorganisms
  • Don't mix with chemical fungicides, pesticides, or herbicides
  • Don't use hot water for initial mixing ‚Äì it kills the culture
  • Don't skip daily stirring ‚Äì ensures even fermentation
  • Don't use metal containers or stirrers ‚Äì plastic or wood only

‚ö†Ô∏è Troubleshooting Common Issues

Problem: Foul Smell

Solution smells rotten instead of fermented, indicating contamination or anaerobic conditions.

Solution

Discard and start fresh. Ensure daily stirring, use clean water, cover loosely (not sealed), and maintain proper jaggery ratio.

Problem: No Fermentation Activity

No foam, bubbles, or activity visible after 3-4 days.

Solution

Check water temperature (too hot or cold), ensure culture is not expired, add more jaggery (500g), and increase stirring frequency.

Problem: Clogged Sprayer/Drip

Nozzles getting blocked when using OWDC in foliar or drip systems.

Solution

Always filter through fine muslin cloth (3-4 layers) before application. Clean filters regularly.
