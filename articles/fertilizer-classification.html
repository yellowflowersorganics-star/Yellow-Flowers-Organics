Understanding Organic Fertilizers and Essential Nutrients for Plant Health

Understanding soil nutrition is key to successful organic farming

Introduction to Plant Nutrition

Plants require 17 essential nutrients for healthy growth and development. Understanding these nutrients and how to provide them organically is fundamental to successful organic farming at Yellow Flowers Organic Farm.

üìä The 17 Essential Nutrients

Macronutrients (Required in Large Quantities)

Primary Macronutrients (NPK)

Nitrogen (N): Promotes vegetative growth, green foliage

Phosphorus (P): Root development, flowering, fruiting

Potassium (K): Overall plant health, disease resistance

Secondary Macronutrients

Calcium (Ca): Cell wall strength, root development

Magnesium (Mg): Chlorophyll production, photosynthesis

Sulfur (S): Protein synthesis, enzyme function

Micronutrients (Required in Small Quantities)

  • Iron (Fe): Chlorophyll formation, enzyme activation
  • Manganese (Mn): Photosynthesis, nitrogen metabolism
  • Boron (B): Cell division, sugar transport
  • Zinc (Zn): Growth hormone production
  • Copper (Cu): Enzyme systems, protein synthesis
  • Molybdenum (Mo): Nitrogen fixation
  • Chlorine (Cl): Photosynthesis, osmotic regulation
  • Nickel (Ni): Enzyme function, nitrogen metabolism

üåø Organic Fertilizer Classification

1. Nitrogen-Rich Fertilizers

  • Jeevamrut: Liquid microbial culture (N-rich)
  • Urine-Based Fertilizers: Gadha Gomutra, OWDC
  • Green Manure: Dhaincha, Sun Hemp
  • Vermicompost: 1-2% N content
  • Neem Cake: 5-6% N, plus pest control

2. Phosphorus-Rich Fertilizers

  • Rock Phosphate: Slow-release P source
  • Bone Meal: 15-20% P content
  • Phosphate Solubilizing Bacteria (PSB): Makes P available

3. Potassium-Rich Fertilizers

  • Wood Ash: 5-7% K content
  • Banana Pseudo-stem: High K levels
  • Seaweed Extract: K plus micronutrients

4. Multi-Nutrient Organic Fertilizers

  • Panchagavya: Complete nutrient solution
  • FYM (Farm Yard Manure): Balanced NPK
  • Compost: 1-2% NPK, improves soil structure

üî¨ Micronutrient Deficiency Recognition

Visual Signs of Deficiency

Iron Deficiency

Yellowing of young leaves (interveinal chlorosis)

Zinc Deficiency

Stunted growth, small leaves, short internodes

Boron Deficiency

Distorted growth, hollow stems, poor fruit set

üß™ Fertilizer Preparation at Yellow Flowers Farm

Jeevamrut Recipe

For 200 liters:

  • 10 kg Fresh cow dung
  • 10 liters Cow urine
  • 2 kg Jaggery
  • 2 kg Pulse flour
  • 1 kg Soil (from bund area)
  • 200 liters Water

Method: Mix all ingredients, ferment for 7 days with daily stirring

Application: Soil drenching at 200L/acre every 15 days

OWDC (Organic Waste Decomposer Culture)

Accelerates composting and provides beneficial microorganisms

Application Rate: 30ml per acre mixed with water and organic matter

üìà Our Nutrient Management Strategy

  1. Soil Testing: Annual testing for NPK and micronutrients
  2. Crop Rotation: Legumes to fix nitrogen naturally
  3. Green Manuring: Before main crop for N enrichment
  4. Regular Applications: Jeevamrut every 15 days
  5. Mulching: Retains moisture and adds organic matter
  6. Microbial Inoculants: Enhance nutrient availability
